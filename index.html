ABOUT ME

I am a junior at the University of Central Florida. My major is Digital Media on the Web Design track and my minor is Computer Science. I am from Boston, Massachusetts and my family currently resides there. I decided to go to school out of state because I wanted to live somewhere warmer and experience new things in life.

Some of my hobbies include shopping, traveling, spending time with my friends, trying new foods, and relaxing at home with my dog, Coco.

PRE - COLLEGE

Before choosing UCF, I went to high school at the Academy for Science and Design. This was a small charter school that specialized in STEM. My high school did not get a lot of funding, therefore they did not have any sports and not many clubs. I felt like I never got a real high school experience and most of my free time went into school or work. Due to the lack of social events at my high school, I did very well in school and finished as the third of my class. My high school also required their students to choose a study of specialization in STEM and we had to take extra classes in that field. At the time, Computer Science fascinated me the most and this was when I realized I wanted to purse a career in this field.

I chose UCF because I wanted a drastic change in my life and I wanted to experience being a student at a large school. I’ve always wanted to go out of state for college and I knew that Florida was perfect for me. Sometimes I get homesick and I miss my family, but I have always been an independent individual so I knew I was capable of making this big move.

FRESHMAN YEAR

I experienced many new life changes during my Freshman year at UCF. I moved from Boston, Massachusetts to Orlando, Florida and this was the farthest away I’ve ever been from home. It took some time for me to adjust to the new environment and not feel homesick. I was originally a Computer Science major, however, after my first Fall semester, I realized it wasn’t what I wanted to pursue. I had a passion for design, specifically through technology. I discovered Digital Media to be the perfect major for me and I continued to keep Computer Science as my minor to enhance my programing skills. Also, all my classes were online because I began college in 2020. These online classes made it possible for me to be in school while being anywhere in the world. I was able to travel to New Orleans, Cancun, and many new cities in Florida during my Freshman year.

JUNIOR YEAR

At the beginning of my Junior year, I began working at a golf course as a beverage cart attendant. I believe this job has benefitted me because I am now a morning person (due to the 6 AM shifts) and I have became more productive with my goals. I decided to no longer be a part of Kappa Delta because I wanted to prioritize my time on school and work. I took a second job as a nail salon receptionist as well. In my Junior year, I learned time management and to spend time with the people I most care for, rather than have superficial friendships. I was able to travel to the Bahamas with my boyfriend and go on many adventures with my best friends. For the rest of my Junior year, I hope on beginning my Honors Undergraduate Research Thesis and find an internship to gain more experience in Digital Media.

FUTURE PLANS

For my second semester of Junior year I intend on beginning my Honors Undergraduate Thesis. This thesis will take about two semesters to complete and I plan on finishing in Fall 2023. I also hope to get an internship for Web Design/Development in Summer 2023. I want to gain work experience in the field to be prepared for when I graduate. I am on track to graduate at UCF in Spring 2024 and I plan on staying in Florida afterwards. I am unsure about what city I will live in, however, it depends on where my future job is. I hope to work for a technology company as a Web Developer or UX/UI Developer.
